Criminal Investigation

BPOC Chapter 32 | TCOLE Preparation

What is Criminal Investigation?

A criminal investigation is the process of legally gathering evidence of a crime that has been or is being committed.

Legal Significance of Evidence

Influence on the Court

The legal significance of evidence rests in its influence on the judge or juror.

Proof Beyond a Reasonable Doubt

The level of certainty a juror must have to find a defendant guilty of a crime. It is a real doubt, based upon reason and common sense after careful and impartial consideration of all the evidence, or lack of evidence, in a case. An investigator must collect corroborating evidence to negate the defense's claims.

Key Terms & Definitions

Bias: A highly personal and unreasoned distortion of judgment.
Crime: An act or omission forbidden by law and punishable by a fine, imprisonment, or even death.
Circumstantial Evidence: Not based on actual personal knowledge or observation, but of other facts from which deductions are drawn.
Complainant: Person requesting an investigation or that action is taken; often the victim.
Corpus delicti: "The body of the crime"; proof establishing that a crime has occurred.
Elements of a crime: Conditions that must occur for an act to be called a specific kind of crime.
Evidence: Anything offered in court to prove the truth or falsity of a fact in issue.
Fact: Something known to be true.
Fence: One who receives and disposes of stolen property on a regular basis.
Field Identification: On-the-scene identification of a suspect by a victim or witness within minutes of the crime.
Prejudice: An opinion or leaning adverse to anything without just grounds or before obtaining sufficient knowledge.
Modus Operandi (MO): A criminal's characteristic method of operation.
Proof beyond a reasonable doubt: Level of proof required to obtain a conviction in a criminal trial.
Probable Cause: Credible information that would cause a reasonable person to believe a suspect committed a crime or that evidence will be found in a particular place.
Rapport: A feeling of ease and harmony in a contact or relationship between people.
Reasonable Doubt: The level of certainty a juror must have to acquit a defendant.
Reasonable Suspicion: Specific articulable facts that would reasonably warrant the detention of a person.
Statement: A legal narrative description of events related to a crime.
Suspect: A person considered to be directly or indirectly connected with a crime.
Witness: A person who saw a crime or has relevant information.
Victim: The person injured by a crime.

Goals of a Criminal Investigator

  • To determine if a crime has been committed.
  • To legally obtain information and evidence to identify the person(s) responsible.
  • To legally arrest the suspect(s).
  • To recover all stolen property.
  • To present the best case possible to the prosecutor.
  • To determine the TRUTH by gathering all information that proves or disproves a person's involvement.

Investigator's Tools

Information

Knowledge gathered from victims, witnesses, suspects, records, reports, and other legitimate sources.

Instrumentation/Forensic Science

Techniques that help in the solution of the crime, such as fingerprints, serology, ballistics, and DNA analysis.

Interviewing

The questioning of victims, witnesses, or suspects in a criminal investigation.

Laws of Arrest, Search, and Seizure

Provides guidance on what investigative techniques are acceptable and can withstand court challenges.

Characteristics of an Investigator

Suspicious: Takes nothing for granted and verifies credibility.
Curious: Desires to investigate and learn facts and truth.
Observant: Trained to take accurate notice using all five senses.
Unbiased & Unprejudiced: Avoids letting personal attitudes lead to sloppy work and incorrect conclusions.
Develops Rapport: Uses interpersonal skills; is patient, courteous, and sympathetic.
Logical Thinking: Applies logic when comparing behavior to statements and evidence.
Creative Problem Solving: Thinks of other ways to accomplish tasks or secure information.
