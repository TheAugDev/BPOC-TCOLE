Child Safety Check Alert List (CSCAL)

BPOC Chapter 19 | TCOLE Preparation

What is the CSCAL? (Family Code ยง261.3022)

The Child Safety Check Alert List (CSCAL) is a tool used by Child Protective Services (CPS) to report the names of children and their legal guardians whom they need to locate. The report is submitted by CPS to the Texas Crime Information Center (TCIC).

Purpose Checklist:

  • To locate a child and guardian to investigate a report of child abuse or neglect.
  • To provide protective services to a family already receiving services.
  • To provide services to the family of a child who is in the state's custody (managing conservatorship).

CSCAL vs. Missing Person Report

Feature Child Safety Check Alert List (CSCAL) Missing Person Report
Child's Status The child is believed to be with a legal guardian. The legal guardian does not know the child's location. The legal guardian can also be DFPS (if the child is in state conservatorship).
Database Entry Entered into **TCIC only** (Texas statewide). Entered into **NCIC** (nationwide).
Duration Remains in TCIC for **12 months**. Remains in the system **indefinitely** until located.

High-Priority Investigations (Family Code ยง261.301)

This statute outlines the protocol for joint investigations between DFPS and law enforcement.

Procedure Checklist:

  • Applies to investigations DFPS has assigned the **highest priority**.
  • If DFPS is unable to locate the child or family, they **must** notify the Department of Public Safety (DPS).
  • If DPS locates the child and family, they **must** notify DFPS of the location.

Practical Example: CPS receives a severe neglect report and gives it the highest priority. After 24 hours, the caseworker still can't find the family at their last known address. Per procedure, CPS notifies DPS to assist in locating the child and family. This may lead to a CSCAL entry.

Possible Encounter Outcomes

An encounter with a person on the CSCAL can result in several different conclusions based on the circumstances and information from DFPS.

Potential Conclusions Checklist:

  • Release the child back to the custody of the legal guardian (most common).
  • Release the child to DFPS/CPS if they respond and decide to take custody.
  • Take temporary possession of the child due to an immediate danger, even for reasons unrelated to the original CSCAL alert.

Initial Encounter Protocol (CCP Art. 2A.056)

When you encounter a person listed on the CSCAL, specific steps are required.

Mandatory Steps Checklist:

  • Immediately contact DFPS on their dedicated law enforcement telephone number.
  • Request information from DFPS regarding the case circumstances.
  • Request information from the child and person regarding the child's safety, well-being, and current residence.

Practical Example: An officer runs a license plate during a traffic stop and receives a TCIC return that the registered owner has a CSCAL entry associated with her child. The officer sees a child in the car. The officer MUST immediately call the DFPS LE-only hotline, inquire about the case, and ask the mother and child about their current residence and the child's well-being.

Authority to Temporarily Detain

CCP Art. 2A.056(d) provides specific authority related to CSCAL encounters.

Detention Authority Checklist:

  • A peace officer **may temporarily detain** the child or other person.
  • The purpose of the detention is **to ensure the safety and well-being of the child**.
  • The CPS caseworker will be notified and will decide whether to respond to the location.
  • Regardless of the CPS response, the officer should assess the child's immediate safety.

Important Note: This authority is for a *temporary* detention to assess safety and complete the required notifications. It is not an arrest. The decision to take the child into possession is a separate, more serious step governed by the Family Code.

Taking Possession in an Emergency (Family Code ยง262.104)

An officer may take temporary possession of a child without a court order ONLY if there is no time to get one and specific conditions are met.

Conditions Checklist (Must meet one):

  • Immediate Danger: Facts (personally known or corroborated) lead a prudent person to believe there is an immediate danger to the child's physical health or safety.
  • Sexual Abuse / Trafficking: Facts (personally known or corroborated) lead a prudent person to believe the child has been a victim of sexual abuse or trafficking.
  • Parental Drug Use: Corroborated information leads a prudent person to believe the parent/guardian is using a controlled substance, and that use constitutes an immediate danger to the child.
  • Methamphetamine Premises: The parent/guardian has permitted the child to remain on premises used for the manufacture of methamphetamine.

Practical Example: During a CSCAL encounter, an officer observes drug paraphernalia next to the child in the car and the parent is clearly intoxicated, creating an immediate danger to the child's physical safety. The officer has grounds under this statute to take temporary possession of the child without a warrant.

Legal Follow-Up After Possession (Family Code ยง262.105)

When an officer takes temporary possession of a child under the emergency conditions of FC ยง262.104, there is a mandatory legal follow-up.

Action Required Checklist:

  • The statute requires that a petition must be filed with the court, usually by DFPS, on the first business day after the child is taken into possession. This initiates the formal legal proceedings.

Officer's Role: While DFPS handles the filing, the officer's detailed and accurate report of the emergency conditions is critical for the petition's success. Your documentation justifies the warrantless removal.

Proper Placement & Release (CCP Art. 2A.058)

If an officer takes possession of a child under Family Code ยง262.104, the child may be released to an authorized person or entity.

Authorized Release Locations Checklist:

  • A residential child-care facility licensed by DFPS.
  • A juvenile probation department.
  • The Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS/CPS).
  • Any other person authorized by law.

Database Checks Before Release to Non-Govt Person

Before releasing a child to a person (other than a government entity), an officer MUST perform several checks.

Mandatory Checks Checklist:

  • Verify with **NCIC** that the child is not listed as missing.
  • Search NCIC databases (protection orders, warrants, sex offender registries) to verify the person does not have a disqualifying order or status.
  • Call the **DFPS Texas Abuse Hotline** to check if the person is listed in the child abuse registry.
  • Verify the person is at least **18 years of age**.
  • Maintain a placement record, including the child's name/pseudonym and the custodian's name and address.

Reporting When Child is NOT Taken (CCP Art. 2A.056(d))

If, after an encounter, you or CPS do not take possession of the child, you still have reporting duties.

DFPS Reporting Checklist:

  • Obtain the child's **current address**.
  • Obtain any other **relevant information**.
  • Report this information to the Department of Family and Protective Services.

Practical Example: After calling the DFPS hotline during a CSCAL stop, it's determined that CPS just needs to verify the family's new address. The officer confirms the address, assesses the child is safe, and does not take possession. The officer must then report the verified address and any other relevant details (like the parent's new phone number) back to DFPS.

Reporting to TCIC to Clear the Alert (CCP Art. 2A.056(e))

After locating a person on the CSCAL and reporting to DFPS, the final step is to clear the TCIC entry.

TCIC Reporting Checklist:

  • Report to the Texas Crime Information Center that the person **has been located**.
  • Report **to whom the child was released** (e.g., back to the guardian at the scene).

Practical Example: Continuing the previous scenario, after the officer provides the updated address to DFPS, their final action is to notify their dispatcher to send a teletype to TCIC to remove the CSCAL entry, noting the child was located and released to the legal guardian on scene.
