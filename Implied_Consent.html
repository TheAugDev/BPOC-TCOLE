Implied Consent (DWI)

Transportation Code Ch. 724 | TCOLE Prep

Consent to Taking of Specimen (TC 724.011)

Legal Definition:

If a person is arrested for an offense arising out of acts alleged to have been committed while the person was operating a motor vehicle in a public place, or a watercraft, while intoxicated, or an offense under Section 106.041, Alcoholic Beverage Code, the person is deemed to have consented... to submit to the taking of one or more specimens of the person's breath or blood for analysis to determine the alcohol concentration or the presence in the person's body of a controlled substance, drug, dangerous drug, or other substance.

Elements Checklist:

  • Person is arrested for an intoxication-related offense (DWI, BWI, etc.).
  • The offense involved operating a motor vehicle in a public place or a watercraft.
  • By operating the vehicle/watercraft, the person is *deemed* to have consented to provide a breath or blood specimen.

Practical Example: A driver is lawfully arrested for DWI after failing sobriety tests. At the station, the officer reads the statutory warnings and requests a breath sample. The driver's prior act of driving on a public road means they have already legally "consented" to this request. A refusal will now carry administrative penalties.

Information Provided by Officer (DIC-24 Warning) (TC 724.015)

Legal Definition:

Before requesting a person to submit to the taking of a specimen, the officer shall inform the person orally and in writing that: (1) if the person refuses, the refusal may be admissible in a subsequent prosecution; (2) if the person refuses, the person's license to operate a motor vehicle will be automatically suspended for not less than 180 days; (3) if the person has a commercial driver's license, refusal will result in disqualification for at least one year; (4) if the officer determines that the person is a resident without a license, the department will deny to the person the issuance of a license for not less than 180 days; (5) if the person is 21 years of age or older and submits to the taking of a specimen and an analysis of the specimen shows the person had an alcohol concentration of 0.08 or more, the person's license will be automatically suspended for not less than 90 days; and (6) if the person is younger than 21 and has any detectable amount of alcohol in the person's system, the person's license will be suspended for not less than 60 days.

Elements Checklist (What the Officer Must Say):

  • Must be provided both orally and in writing.
  • Inform of consequences for **refusing** (admissible in court, 180-day suspension).
  • Inform of consequences for **failing** (90-day suspension for BAC >= 0.08 for adults).
  • Inform of specific consequences for minors and CDL holders.

Taking of Specimen (Mandatory Blood Draw) (TC 724.012)

Legal Definition:

A peace officer SHALL require the taking of a specimen of the person's breath or blood under any of the following circumstances if the officer arrests the person for an offense involving the operation of a motor vehicle or watercraft and the person refuses the officer's request to submit to the taking of a specimen voluntarily...

Elements Checklist (When is a draw MANDATORY after a refusal?):

  • The person was operating a motor vehicle or watercraft.
  • The person was arrested for an intoxication-related offense.
  • The person refused to provide a specimen voluntarily.
  • **AND one of the following conditions exists:**
    • As a result of the accident, another person has died or suffered serious bodily injury.
    • As a result of the accident, another person has suffered bodily injury and been transported to a hospital.
    • The offense was DWI with Child Passenger (PC 49.045).
    • The person has previous convictions for certain intoxication offenses (e.g., two prior DWIs, one prior Intoxication Manslaughter).

Practical Example: An officer arrests a driver for DWI after a crash where the other driver was transported to the hospital with a broken arm (bodily injury). The arrested driver refuses to provide a breath sample. Because the crash resulted in a bodily injury requiring transport to a hospital, the officer shall require a blood draw and can use reasonable force to obtain it without a warrant.

Prohibition on Taking Specimen (TC 724.013)

Legal Definition:

Except as provided by Section 724.012(b) [Mandatory Blood Draw], a specimen may not be taken if a person refuses to submit to the taking of a specimen designated by a peace officer.

Elements Checklist:

  • A person is arrested for an intoxication offense.
  • The officer requests a specimen.
  • The person refuses.
  • **AND** none of the mandatory blood draw conditions from TC 724.012 exist.
  • Result: The officer cannot take the specimen and must proceed with the license suspension process for the refusal.

Practical Example: An officer arrests a driver for DWI after a traffic stop for speeding. There was no crash, no child passenger, and the driver has no prior DWI convictions. The driver refuses to provide a breath sample. The officer cannot force a blood draw; their only recourse is the administrative license suspension for the refusal.

Person Incapable of Refusal (TC 724.014)

Legal Definition:

A person who is dead, unconscious, or otherwise incapable of refusal is considered not to have withdrawn the consent provided by Section 724.011. A specimen may be taken from such a person.

Practical Example: An officer arrives at a single-vehicle rollover crash and finds the driver unconscious but alive. The officer smells a strong odor of alcohol coming from the driver. The driver is transported to the hospital. A blood specimen can be taken at the hospital for analysis because the driver is incapable of refusal.

Procedures for Taking & Analyzing Specimens

Breath Specimen (TC 724.016)

Legal Definition:

A breath specimen must be taken by an individual who is certified by the department to operate the breath analysis instrument. The analysis must be performed under rules of the department.

Blood Specimen (TC 724.017)

Legal Definition:

Only a physician, qualified technician, chemist, registered professional nurse, or licensed vocational nurse may take a blood specimen... The person who takes the specimen may do so on the request or order of a peace officer. The person who takes a blood specimen under this chapter is not liable for damages arising from the request for the specimen if the request is made or the order is issued by a peace officer.

Elements Checklist:

  • Only specified medical personnel can take a blood sample.
  • Medical personnel who take a specimen at the request of an officer are exempt from civil liability.

Furnishing Information to Accused (TC 724.018)

Legal Definition:

On the request of a person who has given a specimen at the request of a peace officer, full information concerning the analysis of the specimen shall be made available to the person or the person's attorney.

Admissibility Rules

Admissibility of Refusal in Civil Action (TC 724.019): A person's refusal to submit to a specimen is NOT admissible in a civil action (like a lawsuit over a car crash).

Admissibility of Refusal of Additional Test (TC 724.063): If a person submits to one test, their refusal to submit to an additional requested test is admissible in court.

Admissibility of Certificate of Analysis (TC 724.064): The results of a breath test, as shown on an analysis certificate, are admissible in a criminal trial.

Administrative License Revocation (ALR) (TC 724.035)

The ALR program is a civil administrative process that is separate from the criminal case. The length of the driver's license suspension depends on the circumstances.

Consequences for REFUSING a Specimen

Refusal is Admissible in Court (TC 724.061)

  • 1st Refusal: 180-day license suspension
  • 2nd or 3rd Refusal (if previous was within 10 years): 2-year license suspension

Consequences for FAILING a Specimen (BAC >= 0.08)

For adults (21+):

  • 1st Failure: 90-day license suspension
  • Subsequent Failure (if previous was within 10 years): 1-year license suspension

For minors (< 21) with any detectable amount of alcohol:

  • 1st Failure: 60-day license suspension
